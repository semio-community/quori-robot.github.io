Research Projects

Reproducible science and studies advancing the field of human-robot interaction

Carnegie Mellon University (Quori Robot Award Program)

Explores social behavior and communication through nonverbal navigation, teachable robot peers for math education, inter-robot social interaction, and embodied multi-agent interaction design using Quori's mobility and expressiveness.

Study

Johns Hopkins University and Hospital (Quori Robot Award Program)

Studies affective interaction, mobility coaching, and long-term field deployments of Quori to support social engagement and physical activity for older adults, producing new datasets and evidence for real-world social robotics.

Study

Oregon State University (Quori Robot Award Program)

Examines robotics and automation for infectious disease response, public perceptions of robot capability, long-term privacy concerns, and integration of Quori into cross-disciplinary education and REU programs.

Study

Saint Louis University and SIU Edwardsville (Quori Robot Award Program)

Develops modular manipulator design guidelines, expressive face rendering, and coordinated face-arm personality embodiment for Quori, evaluated through expanded user studies.

Study

University of California, San Diego (Quori Robot Award Program)

Advances human-centered motion planning, machine learning, and systems engineering for long-term Quori deployments in home and health contexts, emphasizing onboard sensing and unsupervised learning.

Study

University of Nevada, Reno (Quori Robot Award Program)

Studies socially-aware navigation, mid-torso actuation, and collaborative interaction across home, office, hospital, and education environments, emphasizing body language and perceived social intelligence.

Study

University of Pennsylvania Modlab (Quori Robot Award Program)

Builds on the original Quori development collaboration to advance low-cost, accessible human-robot interaction research through the University of Pennsylvania's Modlab.

Study

University of Southern California Interaction Lab (Quori Robot Award Program)

Advances socially assistive robotics with multimodal interaction modeling, expressive communication, social dynamics (trust, empathy, motivation), and long-term user modeling for diverse populations.

Study

University of Washington (Quori Robot Award Program)

Explores Quori as a therapeutic, stress-reducing social robot in the wild through adolescent-robot interaction studies, comparative intervention experiments, and longitudinal ethnographic deployments.

Study

University of Wisconsin-Madison (Quori Robot Award Program)

Integrates formal methods, emotionally sophisticated interaction, and a comprehensive HRI software stack to build foundational capabilities for autonomous social robots on Quori.
